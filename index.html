About

I am a PhD student in the College of Computing at Georgia Tech, advised within Prof. Wenke Lee’s group. My research sits at the intersection of machine learning and security, with recent work on robust learning for autonomous systems and audio-based control/monitoring.

Before Georgia Tech, I spent four years as a researcher at the Technology Innovation Institute (TII) in the UAE, working on ML for UAV security, anomaly detection, and adversarial audio.

Research

  • Robust representation learning for safety-critical autonomy (contrastive/self-supervised objectives such as SimCLR/MoCo) for anomaly detection in UAV flight.
  • Audio-based control & verification for UAVs; studying failure modes and secure interfaces under noise and adversarial manipulation.
  • Adversarial audio & generative attacks for condition monitoring and classification (GANs, zero-/few-shot evaluation).
  • Applied deep learning: spectrogram-based classification, LSTM/CNN pipelines, and embedded ML on Crazyflie AI-deck.

Publications
